WHAT IS NURSING?

 

Nursing is a healthcare profession focused on the care of individuals, families, and communities to help them achieve, maintain, or recover optimal health and quality of life. Nurses play a crucial role in patient care by providing physical and emotional support, administering medications and treatments, monitoring patient health, educating patients and families, and collaborating with other healthcare professionals.

 

KEY ASPECTS OF NURSING

Patient Care: Nurses assess and monitor patients, administer treatments, assist with personal care, and ensure comfort and safety.

Advocacy: Nurses advocate for patients' rights, health needs, and access to quality care.

Education: They educate patients and families about health conditions, medications, and preventive measures.

Collaboration: Nurses work closely with doctors, therapists, social workers, and other healthcare professionals.

Specializations: Nursing includes various specialties such as pediatric nursing, critical care, psychiatric nursing, oncology nursing, and community health nursing.

Ethics and Compassion: Nursing is grounded in strong ethical principles and a deep sense of compassion and empathy.
